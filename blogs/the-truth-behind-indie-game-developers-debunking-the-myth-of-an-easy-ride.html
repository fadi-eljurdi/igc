The Truth Behind Indie Game Developers: Debunking the Myth of an Easy Ride

You have just finished that last line of code; the graphics and visuals are stunning; the user experience is cutting-edge. Everything seems to be in-order. Your excitement however got you a bit carried away, as even though the core mechanics are functioning just fine, your enthusiasm blindfolded you from those micro glitches and bugs that require tweaking. Such amends might seem trivial in the grand scheme of things, but there are much bigger problems that need tackling and some might not necessarily be in the realm of the technical side.

Being a game developer, an independent (indie) game developer for that matter is quite exciting as it means that you are a unique fusion of creativity, technical skills, and problem-solving. Additionally, implementing your vision and the liberating autonomy that accompanies it; being your own boss is just superb. No job however is devoid of hindrances. As like any other job, indie developers have their fair share of challenges.

What obstacles could indie developers face I hear you ask?

Well, they could face an array of problems, even dilemmas at times.

Lack of Motivation

When approaching game development as an indie developer, the whole process could be overwhelming and time consuming. As pursuing project tasks becomes mundane, maintaining a high level of motivation requires dedication to overcoming setbacks and other faced difficulties.

Limited Time

“Rome wasn’t built in a day”, a quote that emphasizes patience to obtain your end-goal. Frustration and surrender could be companions of yours since limited time can slow down your progress and make it hard to meet your development milestones or deadlines, especially if you’re working alone.


Team Assembly

Even though solo play (pun intended) is common in this industry, there comes a time where collaboration and the combined effort of a team becomes mandatory for longer and more composite projects. Finding and hiring a team is not the only headache developers face, but the team’s commitment and passion to the project could directly influence its outcome and trajectory.



Marketing & Visibility

An enticing game with alluring visuals is not enough to attract gamers to buy your game. It simply needs a mighty and aggressive marketing strategy which is crucial to success. Successful and large-scale marketing campaigns necessitate hefty premiums and reaching out to gaming influencers.


Technical Hurdles

Tackling technical issues such as bugs is an inherent part of game development. Such challenges can be a bit frustrating and more importantly, time-consuming, thus disrupting the whole process and the quality of the end product.

Fierce Competition

The indie game market has an extensive list of players, making it a highly competitive industry. Moreover, developing a game that stands out among many other competitors requisites a distinct set of skills and showcasing yourself as distinguishable.

Resources

Game development requires numerous resources that include most and foremost, financial investment, as securing funding to cover development costs can be a significant impediment. Furthermore, development requirements typically include software licenses and proper hardware.

Enhanced Visuals & Gameplay
Back in the day, the gaming audience preferred enjoyable gameplay over stunning graphics. Our modern-day devices however have gone a long way specs-wise and even our preferences regarding visuals and gameplay are ever-evolving. In the present day for instance, a game’s graphics and captivating gameplay seem trivial or in other words a given. Gamers nowadays are demanding more complex and “realistic graphics”, in addition to the rise of artificial intelligence that could be integrated into games, rendering them even more complex.

Supply Vs Demand

“In 2021, the number of Unity creators increased 31% compared to 2020. Additionally, there were 93% more games made on the Unity platform in 2021 than in 2020.”
Every commercial activity out-there abides by the law of supply and demand. Game development is not any different. As we’ve mentioned earlier above, there is an overwhelming large number of developers and projects that exceed the demand of the market.


All in all, being an indie game developer has its ups and downs. It requires strong passion for this industry, patience, and the ability to adapt to be able to carry on with it. A combination of aesthetically pleasing visuals, good gameplay, and integration of artificial intelligence to be able to compete and generate revenue.
