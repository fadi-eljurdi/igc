Breaking Down Steam's Exciting New Pricing Policy: What Gamers Need to Know

Steam to Convert Its Sales Currency to USD in Turkey, Argentina, & Other
Countries

For this week’s blog post, we will be shedding light on Steam’s new pricing policy for both
MENA and LATAM regions.

No, we are not talking about water vapor! We are talking about Steam - a video game digital
distribution service that was developed by Valve.

In Turkey and Argentina, Steam has recently announced that the Steam store will change its
sales currency from the local currency to USD for all of its sales. This change will be effective
starting November 20th, 2023. Pricing will also be updated for both LATAM and MENA regions
which will also include Argentina and Turkey respectively. Steam has additionally
recommended reducing prices for the aforementioned regions and have updated their
suggestions in their pricing tools to reflect this. As a result, some countries might either see
pricing increase or decrease according to what games have been priced prior to the new
currency switch.

What Seems to Be the Issue?

The recent fluctuation in exchange rates in Argentina and Turkey has caused a significant
challenge for game developers in determining appropriate pricing strategies and maintaining
current prices. This issue has been a focal point of discussions during Steam's developer meet-
ups and roundtable sessions. Additionally, Steam has encountered difficulties in ensuring the
continuous operation of payment methods in these countries due to ongoing foreign exchange
fluctuations, fees, taxes, and logistical complications.
Which Exchange Rate will Be Used?
Steam will use the official US Bank exchange rate on November 20th to establish the
exchange rate, through which Steam users’ wallets will automatically convert to USD.

Will the New Regions Have the Same Game Prices As
the USA?

Game cre­ators usually set the price for their game­s on Steam for both the LATAM - USD and
MENA - USD areas. The deadline for providing new pricing for the LATAM & MENA regions will
be Novembe­r 20th. If a developer fails to provide new USD prices by the aforementioned date,
Steam will switch to their de­fault USD cost.
