Level Up Your Bank Account: How to Turn Gaming from a Hobby to a Lucrative Source of Income

It’s More than Just a Hobby… It’s a Livelihood!


Yes, gaming has become more than just a pastime activity to chill, but it has certainly become more than a “time wasting distraction”. Gaming today is a prosperous source of income that is expected to be worth $242.39 billion in 2023, according to Grand View Research.

Simply imagine a world where your favorite pastime not only provides enjoyment but also leads to financial stability, homeownership, and even fame. Well, welcome to the alluring world of gaming, where the community is expected to reach an impressive 3.32 billion players by 2024.
 
Throughout the lines of this article, we will reveal the outstanding evolution of gaming, as it transitions from a mere fun activity into a thriving source of livelihood for thousands of individuals across the globe.

Gaming in our current times is being utilized as a source of livelihood in numerous ways. Some of the most common include:

Streaming:
 
There are gaming content creators that are considered to be at the “top of the food chain” that are now multi-millionaires. At the top of the table, some creators possess a net worth that rivals some actors and musicians, and they live a flashy lifestyle.

Gamers can simply broadcast the games they are playing on websites like Twitch and YouTube, where spectators can support them with donations or channel subscriptions.

Competitive gaming:
 
Competitive gaming is another way for gamers to make money. There have been lots of competitive gaming tournaments but the most prominent would be “Esports”. Esports was originally named by “Kevin Leach” back in 1998, founder of World Cyber Games.
Esports is not a mere tournament that happens in a basement of some teenage amateurs. The industry is real and prospering - (over 380 million people watch esports worldwide both online and in person) as it is a global activity that encompasses skilled video gamers that play competitively; in the same manner as basketball and football competitions are played.

Content creation:
 
Some gamers are able to make money by creating and selling video game content. That includes everything from a game manual to instruction videos, and they can even do video games themselves. For example, some gamers create and sell mods or skins for popular video games.

Game development:
 
Some of the players may be able to make their passion for gaming a career in game development. They basically work on everything from designing to programming and art at all stages of the game development process.


Game Testing:
 
Before being released, games go through various phases of development. That’s why game development companies actually hire individuals to try out their newly developed game and report in with any findings of errors or bugs.


Game media:
 
Some gamers are able to make a living by writing about, talking about, or making videos about video games. They may work for gaming websites or magazines, or they may create their own content channels. IGN for instance, is one of biggest websites and key players in the world, as it possesses a numerous number of editors that craft content and write news about video games.


How Much Do They Make?

Now for the good part! This is for you intrigued individuals out there! How much do gamers actually make? Well, that’s quite a broad question as gamers could be categorized into more than one section. Gamers that participate in global tournaments for instance make literally millions of dollars. Take “The International 2019” Dota 2 tournament for instance, which had a prize pool of over $34 million.

Gamers’ categories include:

Small Streamers

These small twitch streamers make around $1,000 – $1,500 per month per 100 views.

Mid-tier Streamers

Twitch streamers with a bigger audience make roughly around $5,000 to $30,000 monthly per 1,000 and 10,000 views respectively.

Top-tier Streamers

Twitch streamers with a large audience can make around $2.5 per subscriber. Gaules, or Alexandre Borba, for instance is estimated to earn $410,000 monthly from Twitch payout, brand deals, and merchandise sales.



In conclusion, the gaming industry has become more than just a purpose of entertainment, but it has surpassed that level to becoming a momentous industry worthy of investing time and money in due to how significantly it has grown in the past 20 years.
