Blast from the past: How good was the PlayStation 3?

"Even desktop chips nowadays, the fastest Intel stuff you can buy is not by far as powerful as the Cell CPU, but it's very difficult to get power out of the Cell. I think it was ahead of its age, because it was a little bit more like how GPUs work nowadays, but it was maybe not balanced nicely and it was too hard to use. It overshot a little bit in power and undershot in usability, but it was definitely visionary," An interview conducted by Michiel Van Der Leeuw for GameInformer, which dates back to 2019.

The PS3, which is the only PlayStation system to not have sold over 100 million units, is by far the least popular of the Playstation home consoles. It is still ranked fifth overall, behind Nintendo's Wii and its other siblings. Hardware-wise, the console is still highly regarded.

The "STI" alliance, consisting of IBM, Sony, and Toshiba, developed the PS3's architecture, which was significantly ahead of its time. The system made use of a processor platform called the Cell Broadband Engine.

What Is the PS3 Cpu Comprised Of?

The central component is the Power Processing Element, or PPE, which is based on IBM's PowerPC 2.02 ISA (Instruction Set Architecture) and has a dual-core, 3.2GHz CPU.

The platform also used eight co-processors, known as Synergistic Processing Elements, or SPEs, which had a 3.2GHz clock speed. The PS3 stood out from the crowd despite only utilizing six of these eight SPEs. With its Xenon CPU, which was based on a modified PPE design, even the Xbox 360 was outclassed.

A Supercomputer Using Networked PS3s?

In 2010, 1,760 Sony PlayStation 3 systems were in use by the U.S Air Force. The fastest computer in the Department of Defense was known as the "Condor Cluster."

For use in current and upcoming Air Force operations and projects, the computer was meant to be used for artificial intelligence research, pattern recognition, satellite imagery processing, and radar enhancement.

Why use a PS3 instead of an actual computer or imagery processing technology? You might ask. Simply due to the fact that a PlayStation at the time cost $300, and the USAF would have had to pay much more per unit for the newest and greatest imagery processing technology. At a total cost of about $1 million, the PlayStations served as the computer's central component.

PS3 As A Blu-ray & DVD Player

One of the reasons the PlayStation 3 was revolutionary when it was introduced was that it was among the first gaming consoles to come with a Blu-ray disc drive. Larger games and better-quality video playback were made possible by Blu-ray's significantly larger storage capacity compared to DVDs, making this a noteworthy feature at the time.

The PS3's Blu-ray drive allowed users to do more than just play games; it transformed the console into a multimedia powerhouse. Users could utilize the console's features as a complete entertainment system to watch high definition movies and other Blu-ray content. Blu-ray discs' sharpness and clarity made the PS3 a popular option for home entertainment centers in addition to gaming.

Most Legendary PS3 Games

Below is a list of the most popular playstation 3 games in no particular order.

Mass Effect 3

In the action role-playing game Mass Effect 3, you take command of the space hero Commander Shepard as he fights the Reapers, an ancient machine race out to exterminate all organic life, in a last-ditch struggle. The game's branching plotline, character customization options, and fierce combat put the galaxy's destiny in your hands.

LittleBigPlanet

In the endearing platformer LittleBigPlanet, you take on the role of customizable Sackboy or Sackgirl, creatures composed of buttons and sackcloth. Ingenious puzzles, imaginative level design, and a lively online community where players can make and share their own levels are all features of the game.

Uncharted 2: Honor Among Thieves

In the action-adventure game Uncharted 2: Among Thieves, you take on the role of Nathan Drake, a treasure hunter who sets out on a global expedition to locate the fabled city of Shambhala. A lovable cast of characters, stunning set pieces, and exhilarating action sequences are all present in the game.

Skyrim

In the expansive Tamriel universe of Skyrim, an open-world action role-playing game, you can build your own unique character and explore. There are innumerable side missions, intricate RPG mechanics, and an unparalleled sense of freedom and adventure in this game.

Grand Theft Auto 5

GTA V nor any of the other Grand Theft Auto games needs an introduction as everyone has played, seen, or heard of the GTA as some point of their life. In GTA V you take on the roles of three distinct thieves who pull off a number of bold heists in Los Angeles. The game offers a satirical perspective on American culture, over-the-top action, and sandbox-style gameplay.
